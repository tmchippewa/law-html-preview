Turtle Mountain Band of Chippewa Indians Tribal Code.

37.02.010 Crimes Involving "Domestic Violence."

(a) The existing Tribal Code may have previously defined crimes involving domestic violence. The purpose of the Turtle Mountain Domestic Violence Code is to clarify that domestic violence is a separate act punishable separately from the underlying crime. When there is a finding that the following crimes have been committed against a family or household member with the purpose of, or having the effect of, inflicting physical harm or bodily injury or placing the family or household member in imminent fear or apprehension of physical harm or bodily injury, the Turtle Mountain Domestic Violence Code shall also apply:

(1) Homicide Offenses;

(2) Sexual Offenses;

(3) Assault Offenses (Battery, Aggravated Assault, Simple Assault, and Intimidation);

(4) Kidnapping;

(5) Abduction;

(6) Weapons Law Violations;

(7) Terrorizing;

(8) Threats and Intimidation;

(9) Arson;

(10) Breaking and Entering;

(11) Stalking;

(12) Disorderly Conduct;

(13) Harassment;

(14) Destruction of Property;

(15) Trespass;

(16) Burglary;

(17) Vandalism of Property;

(18) Stolen Property Offenses;

(b) Committing one or more of the above stated crimes shall not diminish the seriousness of domestic violence or take precedence over the crime of domestic violence. Under this section, the commission of one of the above referenced crimes against a family or household member with the purpose of, or having the effect of, inflicting physical harm or bodily injury or placing the family or household member in imminent fear or apprehension of physical harm or bodily injury shall trigger the application of the Turtle Mountain Domestic Violence Code. Even if the criminal complaint charges one the above offenses, the person may be charged with the crime of domestic violence and may be punished as provided in Chapter 37.03 of the Turtle Mountain Domestic Violence Code.
