24.13.270 Lessor's Damages for Non-Acceptance or Repudiation

(a) Except as otherwise provided with respect to damages liquidated in the lease agreement (Section 24.1304) or determined by agreement of the parties (Sections 24.1303 and 24.0102(3), if a lessor elects to retain the goods or a lessor elects to dispose of the goods and disposition is by lease agreement, whether or not the lease agreement qualifies for treatment under Section 24.13.260(b), or is by sale or otherwise, the lessor may recover from the lessee as damages for default by the lessee (Section 24.13.220(a)

(1) accrued and unpaid rent as of the date the lessor obtained possession of the goods or such earlier date as the lessee has made an effective tender of possession of the goods back to the lessor,

(2) the present value as of the date determined under Paragraph (a) of the difference between the total rent for the then remaining lease term of the original lease agreement and the market rent at the time determined under Paragraph (a) and at the place where the goods were located on that date computed for the same lease term, and

(3) any incidental damages allowed under Section 24.13.190, less expenses saved in consequence of the lessee's default.

(b) If the measure of damages provided in Subsection (1) of this section is inadequate to put a lessor in as good a position as performance would have, the measure of damages is the present value of the profit, including reasonable overhead, the lessor would have made from full performance by the lessee, together with any incidental damages allowed under Section 24.13.290, due allowance for costs reasonably incurred and due credit for payments or proceeds of disposition.
